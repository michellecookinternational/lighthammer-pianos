The Educated Piano

The Educated Piano is the only professional text that treats developing the feel and tone of a piano simultaneously. Written for practicing technicians, it assumes a foundation in the general principles of piano service and the ability to reason from first principles.

The book draws on Ed's decades of research into the structural relationships that govern piano tone: hammer weight, action leverage, string scaling, duplex interactions, and the acoustic behavior of the soundboard. It is both a theoretical framework and a practical guide, connecting the physics of sound production to the hands-on procedures that produce musical results.

Ed titled it The Educated Piano because the work happens to the instrument itself. The piano is educated through the technician's knowledge, skill, and intention.

Since its publication, the book has become a reference for technicians worldwide who seek to move beyond routine service into the deeper craft of tonal development.
